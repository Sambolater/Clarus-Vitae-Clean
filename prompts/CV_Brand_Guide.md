# Section 1: Brand Foundation

## Brand Story & Origin

**Clarus Vitae** — Latin for "Clear Life" — was founded on a singular conviction: the wellness industry has a trust problem, and affluent individuals making significant health investments deserve better.

The premium wellness market is saturated with marketing-driven claims, undisclosed affiliate relationships, and assessment methodologies that prioritize partnerships over outcomes. For individuals investing $3,000 to $150,000 in wellness experiences, this opacity creates unacceptable risk. Clarus Vitae exists to provide the clarity, intelligence, and discretion that high-stakes wellness decisions demand.

We are not a wellness provider. We are the authority layer — the Michelin Guide of wellness intelligence. We research, evaluate, and document so our audience can make informed decisions with complete confidence.

## Mission

To provide verified, independent intelligence that enables confident decision-making for consequential wellness investments.

## Vision

To establish the definitive standard for wellness facility evaluation — where the Clarus Index becomes the trusted benchmark for outcomes-focused wellness intelligence worldwide.

## Positioning Statement

**"The outcomes intelligence platform for high-stakes wellness decisions. We provide the verified, independent analysis that doesn't exist anywhere else — so you can make decisions with complete clarity."**

This positioning specifies the category (outcomes intelligence), narrows the frame appropriately (high-stakes), addresses the trust gap directly (verified, independent), and connects to brand etymology (clarity).

## Brand Promise

We promise to deliver:
- **Clarity** — Cutting through the confusion of conflicting claims
- **Intelligence** — Providing analysis, not just information
- **Discretion** — Respecting privacy at every level

## Core Values & Principles

### 1. Evidence Over Adjectives
Every claim is backed by specific data points, metrics, or verifiable facts. "Exceptional" means nothing; "1:2.5 physician-to-guest ratio" means everything.

### 2. Transparency Over Mystique
We show our methodology. We acknowledge limitations. We name our experts. Trust is built through openness, not secrecy.

### 3. Substance Over Style
Quality through restraint. We lead with data, not design flourishes. Our authority comes from rigor, not aesthetics.

### 4. Independence Over Revenue
Editorial integrity is non-negotiable. Properties cannot pay for inclusion, ranking, or favorable coverage.

### 5. Privacy Over Convenience
We protect user research and identity absolutely. No tracking, no data sales, no compromises.

|---------|-------------|-----------|----------|
| High-Stakes Health Optimizer | Views health as strategic asset; deep researcher | $20M–$200M | Measurable outcomes |

### Audience Characteristics

Our audience is **marketing-immune**. They have encountered every luxury cliché and respond to signals of genuine expertise and independence rather than promotional language. They:

- Make decisions based on evidence, not aspiration
- Value discretion and privacy highly
- Expect substance over superlatives
- Trust named experts over anonymous claims
- Appreciate transparency about limitations

# Section 3: Brand Personality & Voice

## Brand Archetype

**The Sage** — Trusted advisor who values truth, knowledge, and evidence. Combined with elements of **The Ruler** — authoritative, quality-focused, and premium without ostentation.

## Personality Attributes

- **Authoritative** — Speaks from deep expertise and rigorous methodology
- **Restrained** — Confident without being promotional or enthusiastic
- **Substantive** — Prioritizes depth and evidence over style
- **Transparent** — Shows methodology and acknowledges limitations
- **Discreet** — Respects privacy and avoids flashiness
- **Precise** — Uses specific language and verifiable claims

## Voice Characteristics

The Clarus Vitae voice embodies **"quiet authority"** — assured expertise without promotional enthusiasm.

### Voice Spectrum by Context

| Context | Formality | Key Characteristics | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------------------|---------|
| Homepage Hero | 7/10 | Direct, problem-aware, evidence-teasing | "The Wellness Industry Has a Trust Problem." |
| Property Profiles | 8/10 | Detailed, specific, balanced (strengths + limitations) | "The 64% return rate suggests this continuity works." |
| Editorial | 7/10 | Authoritative, analytical, occasionally pointed | "When language fails, confusion follows." |
| Email | 6/10 | Professional, respectful, no false warmth | "We'd rather maintain your trust than your engagement metrics." |
| Social Media | 6/10 | Substantive but accessible, insight-leading | "Price signals positioning. Evidence signals efficacy." |
| Microcopy | 5/10 | Clear, reassuring, functional | "No tracking. No cookies. No footprint." |

*Formality Scale: 1 = casual/conversational → 10 = formal/institutional*

## Critical Voice Rules

### 1. Lead with Evidence, Not Adjectives
Every claim backed by specific data points, metrics, or verifiable facts. This is the single most important voice principle.

### 2. Acknowledge Limitations Explicitly
Properties have weaknesses; we name them. "No MD on permanent staff," "the alpine location is remote," "outcome tracking is self-reported."

### 3. Never Use Wellness Clichés
Permanently prohibited: "transform your life," "journey to wellness," "curated collection," "world-class," "ultimate luxury," "rejuvenating experience," "unlock your potential."

### 4. Maintain Measured Confidence
No exclamation points. No urgency language. No promotional enthusiasm. We present evidence and let readers draw conclusions.

### 5. Respect the Reader's Intelligence
Explain technical concepts without condescending. Assume sophistication while not assuming specialized expertise.

### 6. Use Consistent Attribution
Expert opinions always attributed to named individuals with stated credentials. Anonymous claims have no place.

### 7. Maintain Structural Clarity
Clear hierarchies and logical organization. In short-form content, make every word count.

## Vocabulary Guide

### Words We Use

| Word/Phrase | Context | Why |
|-------------|---------|-----|
| Intelligence | Our offering | Signals analysis and insight, not just information |
| Verified | Trust signals | Implies we checked; conveys rigor |
| Methodology | Index, evaluation | Technical precision, invites scrutiny |
| Evaluate/Assessment | Our process | Active, critical—not passive curation |
| Proprietary | Index, data | Signals unique value without overselling |
| Substantive | Content quality | Implies depth over flash |
| Discretion | Privacy, service | Respects UHNW sensitivity |
| Evidence | Outcome claims | Science-adjacent without clinical coldness |
| Protocol | Treatment descriptions | Medical precision, professional seriousness |
| Transparent | Methodology, pricing | Directly addresses trust concerns |

### Words We Avoid

| Word/Phrase | Why We Avoid | Alternative |
|-------------|--------------|-------------|
| Transformative | Overused, overpromises | "measurable improvement" or specific outcome |
| World-class | Meaningless superlative | Specific credential or comparison |
| Luxury/luxurious | Generic, expected | Describe the actual experience quality |
| Curated | Wellness cliché | "evaluated," "selected," "assessed" |
| Journey | Wellness cliché | "program," "protocol," "experience" |
| Exclusive | Often false, signals insecurity | Let selectivity speak through data |
| Ultimate | Hyperbolic, empty | Remove entirely |
| Amazing/incredible | Casual enthusiasm | Remove entirely; let facts impress |
| Holistic | Overused, often meaningless | Name specific integrated elements |
| Rejuvenation | Spa cliché | "restoration," "recovery," or specific outcome |
| Pamper | Trivializes the offering | "treatment," "care," "protocol" |
| Escape/getaway | Tourism language | "program," "stay," "immersion" |

# Section 4: Visual Identity

## Design Philosophy

The Clarus Vitae visual system embodies **"quiet authority"** — a premium aesthetic that conveys expertise and exclusivity without ostentation. Every visual element reinforces trust, clarity, and sophisticated restraint.

### Guiding Principles

1. **Substance over style** — Design serves content, never dominates it
2. **Restraint over decoration** — Every element must earn its place
3. **Clarity over complexity** — Information hierarchy is paramount
4. **Premium without pretension** — Quality through materials and execution, not embellishment

### Symbol Rationale

The CV monogram combines the C and V letterforms into an interlocking symbol representing:
- **Clarity** — The open arc of the C suggests transparency
- **Precision** — The geometric V represents exactness
- **Unity** — Overlapping forms suggest integration of wellness dimensions
- **Quiet Authority** — Clean, minimal lines without ornamentation

### Clear Space Requirements

- **Minimum clear space**: Height of the "C" in CLARUS on all sides
- **For symbol**: Equal to the width of one stroke on all sides

### Logo Don'ts

- Do not stretch, distort, or rotate the logo
- Do not change logo colors outside approved palette
- Do not add effects (shadows, gradients, outlines)
- Do not place logo on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast
- Do not recreate or approximate the logo
- Do not use the symbol without the wordmark except in approved contexts (favicon, app icon)

# Section 6: Photography & Imagery

## Photography Philosophy

Images should feel **observed rather than staged**, **substantive rather than aspirational**. This is documentary luxury — showing actual spaces, equipment, and practitioners, not idealized versions.

### Core Principles

1. **Documentary luxury** — Capture real moments, real spaces, real expertise
2. **Authenticity over aspiration** — Show what actually exists
3. **Quality through detail** — Let craftsmanship and materials speak for themselves
4. **Human expertise visible** — Include practitioners to build trust
5. **Restraint in emotion** — Calm, considered compositions
6. **Privacy paramount** — Never show identifiable guests

## Shot Categories

### Property Hero
- **Usage**: Profile headers, featured placements
- **Aspect Ratio**: 16:9
- **Resolution**: 3840 × 2160px minimum (4K)
- **Requirements**: Architectural context, natural light, establishes sense of place

### Treatment Spaces
- **Usage**: Demonstrate clinical capability
- **Requirements**: Equipment visible, practitioner preferred, true white balance
- **Note**: Focus on precision and professionalism, not relaxation imagery

### Technology & Equipment
- **Usage**: Support evidence-based positioning
- **Requirements**: Sharp detail, accurate color reproduction, equipment in context

### Environmental
- **Usage**: Establish setting quality
- **Requirements**: Natural light, material textures, architectural features

### Expert Portraits
- **Usage**: Build trust through visible expertise
- **Requirements**: Environmental setting (not studio), direct eye contact, professional attire

### Detail Shots
- **Usage**: Signal premium quality
- **Requirements**: Tight framing, side lighting for texture, focus on materials and craftsmanship

| Contrast | -10 to -15 | Softer, less harsh |
| Highlights | -20 to -30 | Recover blown areas |
| Shadows | +15 to +25 | Lift for detail |
| Vibrance | -5 to -10 | Slightly desaturated |
| Saturation | -5 to -10 | Muted, not drained |
| Temperature | Neutral to +200K | Slight warmth acceptable |

## Photography Don'ts

- No identifiable guests without explicit release
- No overly staged or artificial-looking compositions
- No heavy filters or dramatic color grading
- No stock photography feeling (generic poses, forced smiles)
- No cluttered or busy backgrounds
- No harsh direct flash
- No excessive post-processing or retouching
- No lifestyle clichés (candles, robes, champagne)

# Section 8: Content Guidelines

## Content Types & Templates

### Property Profile Structure

1. **Header Block**: Property name, location, Index score, verification date
2. **Overview**: 150 words max — positioning, differentiators, program range
3. **Key Metrics Table**: 6-8 quantified data points with Index context
4. **Index Breakdown**: 5 paragraphs (~400 words total) explaining each dimension
5. **What Sets Apart**: 3-4 specific differentiators with explanation
6. **Program Highlights**: 3 signature programs with duration, price, description
7. **Expert Notes**: Attributed quote from named advisor with credentials
8. **Considerations**: 3-5 honest limitations/caveats
9. **Best For**: 4-5 bullets summarizing ideal guest profile

### Editorial Article Structure

1. **Headline**: 5-10 words, insight-leading
2. **Subheadline**: 15-25 words, expands on headline
3. **Lead paragraph**: Sets up the problem or insight
4. **Body**: Evidence-backed analysis, named expert attribution
5. **Conclusion**: Actionable takeaway or perspective

## Editorial Standards

### Checklist for New Content

- [ ] Does every significant claim have specific backing?
- [ ] Have I eliminated prohibited words/phrases?
- [ ] Have I acknowledged relevant limitations?
- [ ] Are expert opinions attributed to named individuals?
- [ ] Does formality match the context?
- [ ] Have I removed vague superlatives?
- [ ] Have I eliminated urgency language?
- [ ] Does the content respect reader intelligence and time?
- [ ] Does it reflect privacy-first principles?
- [ ] Would a sophisticated reader recognize this as Clarus Vitae?

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Correction |
|---------|--------------|------------|
| Leading with superlatives | UHNW audiences are superlative-immune | Lead with evidence |
| Hiding limitations | Undermines trust when discovered | Acknowledge proactively |
| Anonymous expert claims | "Experts say" has no credibility | Name the expert |
| Wellness jargon | Signals lack of substance | Use precise language |
| Promotional enthusiasm | Reads as desperate | Let evidence impress |
| Vague benefits | Sounds generic | Quantify or specify |

## Brand Review Process

### When Review Is Required

- Any new external communication using brand assets
- Any new content type not previously established
- Any partnership or co-branding opportunity
- Any press release or media statement
- Any update to core brand elements

### Review Levels

| Content Type | Review Required |
|--------------|-----------------|

| Editorial article | Editorial team |
| Marketing collateral | Brand manager |
| Press/media | Executive + legal |
| Partnership materials | Executive + legal |

## Brand Absolutes (Never Do)

### Visual Identity
- Never distort, rotate, or modify the logo
- Never use unapproved colors
- Never use unapproved typefaces for brand materials
- Never violate minimum clear space requirements
- Never place logo on busy/low-contrast backgrounds

### Voice & Content
- Never use prohibited vocabulary (see Section 3)
- Never make claims without evidence
- Never cite anonymous sources
- Never use urgency or promotional language
- Never hide limitations or caveats

### Editorial Independence
- Never accept property advertising
- Never let revenue influence assessments
- Never publish content paid for by evaluated properties
- Never provide preferential treatment based on relationships

### Privacy
- Never track users without consent
- Never sell or share user data
- Never reveal user research or identities
- Never compromise Research Mode privacy

## Risk Considerations

### Top Brand Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| High-rated property incident | Medium | Very High | Continuous monitoring, rapid response protocols |
| Editorial independence questioned | High | High | Structural commitments, preemptive transparency |
| Major competitor entry | Medium | High | Build moats quickly, emphasize independence |
| Methodology criticized | Medium | Medium-High | Publish methodology, continuous refinement |
| Content quality inconsistency | High | High | Quality controls, capacity-constrained growth |

### Crisis Response Framework

**Property Incident Response:**
1. Immediately pause all promotional mentions of property
2. Initiate review of current Index score and assessment
3. Prepare transparent communication about review process
4. Update assessment based on new information
5. Document changes and reasoning publicly

**Editorial Independence Challenge:**
1. Review specific allegation with full documentation
2. Prepare detailed response with evidence
3. Consider third-party audit if appropriate
4. Communicate transparently with audience
5. Strengthen structural commitments if gaps identified

## Quality Controls

### Content Quality Standards

- All property assessments require named expert review
- All claims require documented evidence
- All expert credentials require verification
- All content requires editorial review before publication

### Capacity-Constrained Growth

- Never expand coverage faster than expert capacity supports
- Never compromise assessment depth for breadth
- Never sacrifice quality for speed
- Never let demand pressure override standards

## Evolution & Update Process

### Brand Guide Updates

- **Quarterly review**: Minor updates, clarifications
- **Annual review**: Comprehensive assessment, potential refinements
- **As-needed**: Urgent corrections or additions

### Change Documentation

All brand guide changes must be:
- Documented with rationale
- Reviewed by brand steward
- Communicated to all stakeholders
- Reflected in all brand assets
